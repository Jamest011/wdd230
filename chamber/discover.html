Geary City Demographics

There are 837 residents in Geary, with a median age in Geary is 32.8 years, 32.7 years for males, and 32.9 years for females. The racial composition of Geary is: 63% White, 20% Native American, 12% more than one race, 3% Black or African American and 2% Native Hawaiian or Pacific Islander.

On average, the household income in Geary is $50,776.

A total of 46% people in Geary are married and 54% are not married.

About 36% of residents hold a highschool diploma, 38% have attended some college, 8% have a bachelors degree and 2% have a graduate degree. The rest of the residents have received some other form of education.

The median rental costs in recent years comes to $546 per month, and the median house value is $60,700. 55.6% of residents own their home and 44.4% of residents rent.

About Our Little Town


Geary is a small farming community with a rich and diverse history. We are home to the only museum in Blaine County, which is housed in the Gillespie Building. The Gillespie building was built in 1903 as the Bank of Commerce, the oldest bank in Blaine county. We are located inside the Cheyenne-Arapaho Nation and have a very active Native community.

We are the home to the final resting place of Arapahoe Chief Left Hand and famed scout and developer of the Chisholm Trail, Jesse Chisholm. They are both buried at Left Hand Springs, just a few miles north of Geary city limits. Geary was also on the original tract of Route 66. We are also home to the "Nation's Oldest & Toughest Wrestling Tournament" which was started in 1945.
